Interoperable Cross-Chain Wallet

Intent‑Centric Cross‑Chain UX

To showcase how cross-chain transaction can feel simple and natural by turning complex infrastructure—like the Ethereum Interop Layer — into an intuitive wallet experience. This includes identifying common user pain points, designing a cross-chain send flow across L2 rollups, unifying balances, and applying intent-based design principles so users can focus on what they want to do, not how the chains work.

Role

  • Web3 product design / concept storytelling

Final UI and storyboards showing intent-based cross-chain flows made simple and intuitive.

DISCOVERY


The cross-chain transaction was technically complex and challenging to explain to product and stakeholder teams, so I used storyboards and early concepts to illustrate user intent, simplify flows, and align everyone on goals.

I set out on the followings:

  • Mapped existing wallet UX and pain points, highlighting fragmentation and trust issues.
  • Defined a UX brief with behavior goals, assumptions, and success metrics.
  • Created concept storyboards for intent-based actions
  • Iterated on flows, mental models, and UI patterns through design explorations.
  • Used AI prototypes to quickly validate ideas and system behaviors.
  • Validated prototypes with internal testing, measuring learnability to track improvements.
  • Collaborated with tech and product teams to ensure feasibility and alignment.

Design Brief


The Problem Space

The cross-chain ecosystem is creating friction for both teams and users. While L2 rollups unlock scalability, they also fragment the experience. Users face confusing flows, multiple approvals, and uncertainty due to reliance on third-party bridges.

  • Ecosystem: Multiple isolated L2s,and dependency on 3rd-party bridges.
  • User: Fragmented wallet experience, cumbersome bridging steps, and trust concerns.

hypotheses

The design aims to change users' manual chain management behaviors to intuitive intent expression.
Hypothesis: HMW we replace manual multi-step chain management with a system that interprets high-level user intents (e.g., "swap across chains") and handles fulfillment, then users can move from fragmented workflows to seamless single-network interactions.

Success Metrics

After agreed with stockholders, they are our success metrics:

  • Cognitive Load: ≤ 3 steps
  • Learnability: ≥ 90% first-time success, ≥ 95% complete without help
  • Approval & Friction: ≥ 95% task completion with one-signature flow
  • System Reliability: ≥ 98% transaction success,

One unified cross-chain send that feels like a single chain action.

Solution


Through research and exploration, I found that users think in terms of intent—sending or holding assets—rather than selecting chains. Current wallets force early chain decisions, creating confusion, and fragmented balances. To address this, I explored intent-driven cross-chain flows, and balance-first information structures. Using USDC as a constraint, I storyboarded 1-to-1 and 1-to-N scenarios to test how the system could abstract complexity while maintaining transparency and user control.

Here’s what the final design achieves:

  • Intent-first: users send assets by goal; routing is handled automatically.
  • Unified balances: view assets as one balance.
  • Consistent cross-chain transfers: supports 1→1 and N-1 flows using USDC
  • Routing and fees are visible only when needed.
  • No chain/bridge knowledge required to act confidently.
  • Simple for non-technical users, fully non-custodial by design.

Impact


I've been working on this project around 5 months. By reframing complex blockchain mechanics into intent-driven stories, this design translates technical complexity into clear, user-centered product direction. The narrative approach unlocked shared understanding across design, product, and engineering, helping teams align on real user problems and roadmap opportunities. Through proof-of-concepts, stakeholders could see how the solution integrates into the wallet ecosystem. This reduced ambiguity, accelerated decision-making, and established UX principles that guide cross-functional collaboration.
